# Steering Ball Joint Manufacturing Process Comparison and Selection Guide

Process selection is a key decision in steering ball joint manufacturing, directly affecting product accuracy, efficiency, cost, and quality. In the global automotive industry, process selection must comply with international standards to ensure product quality meets **IATF 16949** quality management requirements. This article systematically introduces steering ball joint manufacturing process comparison and selection guide from dimensions of traditional process analysis, advanced process analysis, process comparison analysis, equipment comparison analysis, application scenario analysis, selection recommendations, upgrade and transformation solutions, and international process selection cases.

## 1. Traditional Process Analysis

### 1.1 Traditional Process Flow

**Process Flow:**

1. **Material Preparation**: Select materials
2. **Forging**: Forge blanks
3. **Turning Processing**: Turn ball head using lathe interpolation
4. **Rolling Processing**: Polish using roller extrusion
5. **Surface Treatment**: Surface treatment
6. **Assembly**: Assembly
7. **Inspection**: Quality inspection

**Process Characteristics:**

- **Mature Process**: Process technology mature
- **Simple Equipment**: Relatively simple equipment
- **Lower Cost**: Lower equipment cost
- **General Accuracy**: General processing accuracy

### 1.2 Traditional Process Characteristics

**Advantages:**

- **Low Cost**: Low equipment investment cost
- **Mature Technology**: Mature and reliable process technology
- **Simple Maintenance**: Relatively simple equipment maintenance
- **Strong Adaptability**: Strong adaptability

**Disadvantages:**

- **Insufficient Accuracy**: Relatively low processing accuracy
- **Low Efficiency**: Low processing efficiency
- **Poor Consistency**: Poor batch consistency
- **High Maintenance Cost**: Frequent maintenance required

### 1.3 Traditional Process Problems

**Accuracy Problems:**

- **Roundness Accuracy**: 0.01–0.02 mm, difficult to achieve high accuracy requirements
- **Surface Roughness**: Ra 0.4–0.8 μm, general surface quality
- **Poor Consistency**: Poor batch consistency

**Efficiency Problems:**

- **Long Processing Time**: Long processing time
- **Low Equipment Utilization**: Low equipment utilization
- **Limited Capacity**: Relatively limited capacity

**Maintenance Problems:**

- **Accuracy Wear**: Mechanical accuracy wears with long-term use
- **Mold Deformation**: Local deformation of polishing pressure roller
- **High Maintenance Cost**: Frequent maintenance and mold replacement required

### 1.4 Traditional Process Cost

**Cost Composition:**

| Cost Item | Proportion | Description |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| **Equipment Cost** | 30% | Equipment investment cost |
| **Labor Cost** | 25% | Labor operation cost |
| **Material Cost** | 20% | Material cost |
| **Maintenance Cost** | 15% | Equipment maintenance cost |
| **Other Costs** | 10% | Other costs |

**Cost Characteristics:**

- **Low Initial Investment**: Lower equipment investment cost
- **High Operating Cost**: Higher operating and maintenance costs
- **Medium Total Cost**: Total cost at medium level
