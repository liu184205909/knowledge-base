# Steering Ball Joint Quality Control System and Testing Methods

Quality control system is the core guarantee of steering ball joint manufacturing, directly affecting product reliability and safety. In the global automotive industry, quality control systems must comply with international standards to ensure product quality meets **IATF 16949** quality management requirements. This article systematically introduces the quality control system of steering ball joints from dimensions of quality control system, quality inspection standards, quality inspection methods, quality inspection equipment, quality improvement methods, and quality certification.

## 3. Quality Inspection Methods

### 3.1 Material Inspection Methods (International Standards)

**Chemical Composition Inspection:**

- **Inspection Method**: Spectral analysis, chemical analysis
- **Inspection Standard**: SAE J403, EN 10083
- **Inspection Items**: C, Cr, Mo and other element content

**Mechanical Performance Inspection:**

- **Inspection Method**: Tensile test, impact test
- **Inspection Standard**: ISO 6892, ISO 148
- **Inspection Items**: Tensile strength, yield strength, impact toughness

**Metallographic Structure Inspection:**

- **Inspection Method**: Metallographic microscope observation
- **Inspection Standard**: ISO 4967
- **Inspection Items**: Structure type, grain size

### 3.2 Dimensional Inspection Methods

**Roundness Inspection:**

- **Inspection Method**: Roundness tester inspection
- **Inspection Standard**: ISO 1101
- **Inspection Accuracy**: 0.001 mm

**Position Inspection:**

- **Inspection Method**: Coordinate measuring machine inspection
- **Inspection Standard**: ISO 1101
- **Inspection Accuracy**: 0.002 mm

**Dimensional Inspection:**

- **Inspection Method**: Caliper, micrometer inspection
- **Inspection Standard**: ISO 2768
- **Inspection Accuracy**: 0.01 mm

### 3.3 Performance Inspection Methods

**Fatigue Performance Inspection:**

- **Inspection Method**: Fatigue testing machine testing
- **Inspection Standard**: ISO 1143, SAE J492
- **Inspection Items**: Fatigue life, fatigue strength

**Environmental Adaptability Inspection:**

- **Inspection Method**: Environmental test chamber testing
- **Inspection Standard**: ISO 16750
- **Inspection Items**: Temperature adaptability, humidity adaptability

**Corrosion Resistance Inspection:**

- **Inspection Method**: Salt spray test
- **Inspection Standard**: ISO 9227
- **Inspection Items**: Corrosion resistance performance

### 3.4 Environmental Adaptability Inspection (International Standards)

**Low Temperature Testing:**

- **Test Temperature**: -40℃
- **Test Standard**: ISO 16750
- **Test Items**: Rotation flexibility

**High Temperature Testing:**

- **Test Temperature**: 120℃
- **Test Standard**: ISO 16750
- **Test Items**: Rotation flexibility

**Salt Spray Corrosion Testing:**

- **Test Conditions**: 5% NaCl solution, 35℃
- **Test Standard**: ISO 9227
- **Test Time**: 24–96 hours

### 3.5 Non-Destructive Testing Methods

**Ultrasonic Testing:**

- **Inspection Method**: Ultrasonic flaw detection
- **Inspection Standard**: ISO 17640
- **Inspection Items**: Internal defects

**Magnetic Particle Testing:**

- **Inspection Method**: Magnetic particle flaw detection
- **Inspection Standard**: ISO 9934
- **Inspection Items**: Surface defects

**Penetrant Testing:**

- **Inspection Method**: Penetrant flaw detection
- **Inspection Standard**: ISO 3452
- **Inspection Items**: Surface defects
