@@@AI_CRYSTAL_MOONSTONE@@@
<p><strong>Moonstone</strong> belongs to the feldspar group and exhibits adularescence, a soft optical glow caused by alternating microscopic layers of orthoclase and albite that scatter light differently depending on viewing angle. Historically, this mineral was linked to lunar cycles and seasonal rhythms across Mediterranean and South Asian cultures, often carried to support emotional equilibrium. In contemporary mindfulness practice, many find its gentle visual resonance useful for breath-pacing exercises and nervous system regulation. When worn or held, it can serve as a tactile reminder to move through emotional shifts with measured patience rather than force.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_SELENITE@@@
<p><strong>Selenite</strong> is a transparent variety of gypsum crystallizing in the monoclinic system, noted for its perfect basal cleavage and ability to polish smoothly without abrasive compounds. Ancient Egyptian communities associated this mineral with lunar illumination and purification, frequently placing it in resting chambers and sacred spaces. Today, practitioners sometimes use it during quiet reflection sessions to establish a clear mental boundary before journaling or grounding work. Its cool, smooth surface offers a straightforward sensory point of contact, encouraging slower thought patterns and a deliberate pause amid daily demands.</p>
