@@@AI_CRYSTAL_MOONSTONE@@@
<p><strong>Moonstone</strong> forms from intergrown orthoclase feldspar, a mineral that exhibits adularescence—a soft glowing effect created by light scattering between alternating crystal layers. Traditional systems have long linked this stone to lunar phases and emotional equilibrium across various cultures. In contemporary mindfulness practice, holding or wearing moonstone during evening wind-down routines can serve as a sensory cue to slow breathing and acknowledge feelings without judgment. For those exploring this placement, the stone’s gentle luminescence often aligns with the need for quiet reassurance rather than abrupt shifts.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_SELENITE@@@
<p><strong>Selenite</strong> consists of hydrated calcium sulfate crystals that cleave cleanly into smooth, translucent plates and generate static charge when rubbed. Historical traditions have associated it with purification and mental clarity, though modern usage emphasizes its physical consistency as a grounding tool. Mindfulness practitioners frequently place selenite near meditation spaces or desk surfaces to create a reliable visual anchor, reminding them to return to steady breath when emotional intensity rises. This placement may benefit from the stone’s straightforward structure, which mirrors the value placed on clear boundaries and uncluttered internal space.</p>
