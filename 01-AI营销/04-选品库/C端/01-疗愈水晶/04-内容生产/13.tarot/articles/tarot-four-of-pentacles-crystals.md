# Four of Pentacles: Tarot Card Meaning & Best Crystals for Self-Reflection

The **Four of Pentacles tarot** is the card of Earth's holding pattern — a figure in a cap and rich robe crouching on a stone bench, one pentacle clutched tight to his chest, another balanced precariously on his head, one under each foot, the city behind him he cannot quite leave. As the suit's **Holder of What He Has**, this is Earth's gathered resources being held — sometimes wisely, sometimes in a grip that has become the cage. On this page you'll find the full Four of Pentacles tarot card meaning (upright and reversed), its yes/no answer, love and career readings, the **best crystals for Four of Pentacles** across five roles, and an Eastern lens on the miser whose wealth owns him.

## Four of Pentacles at a Glance

- **Yes / No (upright):** **Maybe** — a holding maybe; the card favours security and saving while warning against the clutch that cannot enjoy what it holds.
- **Yes / No (reversed):** **Yes** — the grip is loosening; generosity is returning; resources are beginning to flow.
- **Archetype:** The Holder of What He Has.
- **Core message:** security and stewardship, or possessiveness that has become the cage.
- **Best crystal overall:** Pyrite (Earth, abundance / will / protection).

## The Suit of Pentacles: Earth Energy

Pentacles is the **Earth** suit — matter, abundance, practice, body, and resources. The suit's whole arc traces the journey of the manifest: from the first opportunity (the Ace), through the juggling of the Two, the collaboration of the Three), the holding of the Four, the scarcity of the Five, the generosity of the Six, the patience of the Seven, the mastery of the Eight, the self-sufficiency of the Nine, and the legacy of the Ten. The Four of Pentacles is the suit's **holding node** — Earth's resources gathered and being held, the moment the build has been consolidated into a grip.

## Four of Pentacles Upright: Meaning & Message

A figure in a cap and rich robe crouches on a stone bench, one pentacle clutched tight to his chest, another balanced precariously on his head, one under each foot; behind him the city he cannot quite leave. The Four of Pentacles is Earth's holding pattern: the moment the resources of the Ace and the build of the Three have been gathered and are now being clutched — sometimes wisely, sometimes in a grip that has become the cage.

Read it slowly and the card's whole teaching appears: **he cannot leave the city — the wealth has become the holder rather than the held.** Upright, this card honours genuine financial security and the discipline of saving, while gently observing the line where security has crossed into possessiveness that cannot enjoy what it holds. The figure is not bad for holding; he is simply stuck in a posture where the holding has become the point.

Upright, the Four of Pentacles does not shame the saving; it acknowledges that building security is real work. Its teaching is simply that *the clutch has a cost*, and that the resources held too tightly cannot do what resources are for — sustain, nourish, grow, be enjoyed. The card's quiet invitation is to ask whether the holding is stewardship or fear.

## Four of Pentacles and the Number 4 in Tarot

Every number carries its own teaching across the four suits, and the number 4 in the Minor Arcana is the **stability / consolidation** node. But the *flavour* of that stability depends entirely on which suit it lands in.

- **Four of Wands (Fire):** celebration and milestone; the first festival after the launch.
- **Four of Cups (Water):** apathy and discontent; the heart refusing the offered cup.
- **Four of Swords (Air):** rest and recuperation; the mind lying down to recover.
- **Four of Pentacles (Earth):** holding and possessiveness; the clutch on what has been gathered.

The Four of Pentacles is unmistakably its own card: the crouching figure, the four pentacles distributed over body and head and feet, the city behind. Where the Four of Wands celebrates and the Four of Swords rests, **the Four of Pentacles clutches** — and that clutching is the suit's specific Earth-way of carrying the number 4, the body's stability turned to hoarding.

## Four of Pentacles Reversed: The Shadow Aspect (Loosening)

Reversed, the same grip begins to loosen — generosity returning, control releasing, scarcity loosening its hold, the willingness to hold resources open-handed rather than clutched to the chest. The figure has begun to stand up from the bench; the question is whether the loosening is wise generosity or reckless abandon.

The shadow of the upright was the clutch; the gift of the reversal is the trust that lets abundance flow rather than freeze. The Four of Pentacles reversed is an invitation, not a curse: *which of your holdings has become more about fear of loss than enjoyment of what is held, and what open-handed posture is the situation asking for?* This is gentle shadow work — the slow willingness to let the grip loosen without throwing the resources away.

## Four of Pentacles in Love, Career & Finances

### Four of Pentacles in Love (Upright)

In love readings, the Four of Pentacles signals possessiveness or holding too tight — jealousy, control, a relationship guarded rather than enjoyed. The card asks whether the grip is protecting the bond or suffocating it.

### Four of Pentacles in Love (Reversed)

Reversed in love: the grip loosening, generosity returning, the relationship re-engaged with open hands.

### Four of Pentacles in Career (Upright)

In career, this card points to holding position tightly — security prized over growth, territory guarded, the role that has become a fortification rather than a foundation.

### Four of Pentacles in Career (Reversed)

Reversed in career: releasing the territorial hold, opening to collaboration or growth.

### Four of Pentacles in Finances (Upright)

For finances, the Four of Pentacles can signal healthy saving and security OR — shadow — hoarding that prevents growth. Distinguish stewardship from clutching; this is strategic framing, not specific investment advice.

### Four of Pentacles in Finances (Reversed)

Reversed in finances: scarcity loosening, resources flowing, savings moving from frozen hoarding to alive stewardship. (Not investment advice — for major financial decisions, consider qualified professional counsel.)

## Four of Pentacles in the Eastern Tradition

The Four of Pentacles resonates with the East Asian cautionary image of **守财奴** (*shǒu cái nú* — "the miser") and the Buddhist observation that grasping (*upādāna*) is itself the suffering. In the classical Chinese tradition, the miser is one of the stock cautionary figures — not because wealth is bad but because the one whose wealth owns him has reversed the proper order of things. The Buddhist observation sharpens the point: the attachment itself, before any external loss, is already the suffering the miser is trying to prevent. The Four of Pentacles' crouching figure clutching his four pentacles is, in his upright posture, exactly this cautionary image; in his reversed posture, he is the slow, honest willingness to reverse the order.

Pyrite has its own cross-tradition lineage here — historically treasured across both Western and Eastern folk practice as a stone of true abundance (rather than its surface appearance of "fool's gold"), the stone that asks the holder to distinguish real wealth from the defensive clutching that merely looks like wealth. The pairing is natural: a holding card meets a true-abundance stone, and the grip becomes a little more likely to be stewardship than fear.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is the Four of Pentacles a yes or no card?

Upright, the Four of Pentacles is a holding **maybe** — the card favours security and saving while warning against the clutch that cannot enjoy what it holds. Reversed, it leans **yes**: the grip is loosening and generosity is returning.

### What does the Four of Pentacles mean in love?

In love, the Four of Pentacles signals possessiveness or holding too tight — jealousy, control, a relationship guarded rather than enjoyed. The card asks whether the grip is protecting the bond or suffocating it.

### Is the Four of Pentacles a bad card?

No. The Four of Pentacles is a holding card, not a bad omen. Even reversed it is not a curse — it simply observes that the grip is loosening and invites honest reflection on whether the holding was stewardship or fear.

### What is the difference between Four of Pentacles and Four of Wands?

Both carry the number 4 — stability / consolidation — but their *flavour* differs entirely. The Four of Pentacles **clutches what has been gathered** (security prized over enjoyment, the miser's grip); the Four of Wands **celebrates a milestone with community** (the floral canopy, the dancers). One holds tight; the other raises wands in joy.

### Which crystal is best for Four of Pentacles?

**Pyrite** is the best overall crystal for the Four of Pentacles — its Earth-element true-abundance quality matches the card's holder-of-what-he-has archetype. For the daily wise stewardship, wear Jade.
