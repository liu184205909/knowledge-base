# Six of Cups: Tarot Card Meaning & Best Crystals for Self-Reflection

The **Six of Cups tarot** is the card of Water turned tender toward the past — in a sunlit town of flowered cups, an older figure in a white tunic accepts a cup brimming with blossoms from a smaller child, six cups filled with flowers arranged around them. As the suit's **Tender Memory**, this is the moment nostalgia softens rather than grieves: the inner child met with kindness, an old friend or place or version of the self offering a gift. On this page you'll find the full Six of Cups tarot card meaning (upright and reversed), its yes/no answer, love and career readings, the **best crystals for Six of Cups** across five roles, and an Eastern lens on meeting the younger self with kindness.

## Six of Cups at a Glance

- **Yes / No (upright):** **Yes** — a tender, nostalgic yes; the card favours warmth, reunion, and the past offering a gift.
- **Yes / No (reversed):** **Maybe** — the nostalgia has curdled into stuckness or is finally releasing; the card advises discerning which.
- **Archetype:** The Tender Memory.
- **Core message:** genuine warmth toward what was; the inner child met with kindness.
- **Best crystal overall:** Moonstone (Water, intuition / new beginnings / inner-child work).

## The Suit of Cups: Water Energy

Cups is the **Water** suit — emotion, intuition, relationship, and the creativity of the heart. The suit's whole arc traces the journey of a feeling: from the first awakening (the Ace), through the first partnership (the Two), the celebration with friends (the Three), the apathy of the Four, the grief of the Five, the nostalgia of the Six, the illusion of the Seven, the leaving of the Eight, the fulfilment of the Nine, and the family joy of the Ten. The Six of Cups is the suit's **nostalgia node** — Water turned tender toward the past, the heart softened by genuine remembrance.

## Six of Cups Upright: Meaning & Message

In a sunlit town of flowered cups, an older figure in a white tunic accepts a cup brimming with blossoms from a smaller child; six cups filled with flowers are arranged around them, and the air is one of gentle, generous remembering. The Six of Cups is Water turned tender toward the past: the moment nostalgia softens rather than grieves, the inner child met with kindness, an old friend or place or version of the self offering a gift.

Read it slowly and the card's whole teaching appears: **the older figure accepts the cup from the smaller child — the adult self receiving the gift of the child self.** Upright, this card honours genuine warmth toward what was — the childhood memory that still nourishes, the reunion that rekindles innocence, the kindness given freely as a child gives. The nostalgia here is not the grief of the Five; it is the softening that follows grief, the heart's willingness to receive what the past still offers.

Upright, the Six of Cups is one of the deck's gentlest cards. It does not promise a return to childhood; it promises that *the past still carries gifts*, and that meeting it with kindness rather than judgement can nourish the present. The card's quiet teaching is that the inner child is not a wound to be healed but a gift to be received — and that the adult self earns its adulthood partly by learning to accept what the smaller self is still offering.

## Six of Cups and the Number 6 in Tarot

Every number carries its own teaching across the four suits, and the number 6 in the Minor Arcana is the **harmony / resolution / mutual exchange** node — the moment the conflict of the 5 finds its softer resolution. But the *flavour* of that harmony depends entirely on which suit it lands in.

- **Six of Wands (Fire):** public victory; recognition, the contest resolved.
- **Six of Cups (Water):** nostalgia and tender memory; the heart turned kindly toward the past.
- **Six of Swords (Air):** passage to calmer water; the mind ferried toward steadier ground.
- **Six of Pentacles (Earth):** generosity and balanced giving; the held resources offered fairly.

The Six of Cups is unmistakably its own card: the sunlit town, the flowered cups, the older and younger figures, the blossoms. Where the Six of Wands is crowned in public and the Six of Swords boards a boat, **the Six of Cups remembers** — and that remembering is the suit's specific Water-way of carrying the number 6, the heart's harmony with what was.

## Six of Cups Reversed: The Shadow Aspect

Reversed, the same nostalgia either curdles into stuckness — idealising the past to avoid the present, refusing to grow up, the memory becoming a refuge from life — or begins to release its grip, the figure finally able to leave childhood behind with gratitude rather than grief. The flowered cups are still there; the question is whether they are being received as gift or refuge.

The shadow here is **not** the remembering itself (the tender warmth was always the gift). The shadow is the unwillingness to let the past be the past — the nostalgia that has become a hiding place from the present, the inner-child work that has become an end in itself rather than a resource for adult life. The Six of Cups reversed invites an honest question: *which memory have you been living inside rather than learning from, and what forward step is the tenderness actually asking to free?* This is an invitation to reflect, not a curse — reversed, the card simply marks that the nostalgia is being tested on whether it serves the present or hides from it.

## Six of Cups in Love, Career & Finances

### Six of Cups in Love (Upright)

In love readings, the Six of Cups signals tenderness, nostalgia, and often reunion — a reconnection with someone from the past, or a relationship whose warmth carries the innocence of an earlier time. Real sweetness; ensure it is meeting the present, not replacing it.

### Six of Cups in Love (Reversed)

Reversed in love: stuck in an idealised past connection, refusing the present relationship for a memory, or nostalgia finally releasing toward something new. The card asks whether the past is being honoured or hidden in.

### Six of Cups in Career (Upright)

In career, this card can point to returning to a beloved earlier path, a mentor or place from the past, or work that reconnects you to what you loved before the world complicated it.

### Six of Cups in Career (Reversed)

Reversed in career: refusing to grow past an early chapter, or — gift — finally leaving a nostalgic role with gratitude and moving forward.

### Six of Cups in Finances (Upright)

For finances, the Six of Cups can signal a gift from the past — an inheritance, a returning favour, a resource remembered and reclaimed. Strategic framing, not specific investment advice.

### Six of Cups in Finances (Reversed)

Reversed in finances: clinging to a past financial arrangement, or releasing it toward a more current strategy. (Not investment advice — for major financial decisions, consider qualified professional counsel.)

## Six of Cups in the Eastern Tradition

The Six of Cups resonates with the East Asian image of **童心** (*tóng xīn* — "child-heart", the uncalculated sincerity of the child) and the practice of meeting one's younger self with kindness rather than judgement. In both the Daoist and Chan Buddhist traditions, *tóng xīn* is not naïveté to be outgrown but a quality to be recovered — the original sincerity of the heart that gets covered over by the strategies of adult life and can be re-met, in maturity, as a gift rather than a lack. The Six of Cups' older figure accepting a cup from a smaller child is, in its own Western way, exactly this image: the adult self receiving what the child self is still offering.

Moonstone has its own cross-tradition lineage here — historically treasured across both Indian and East Asian traditions as a stone of the moon's receptive, reflective principle (Chandra / Soma), the stone of inner-child work and the gentle memory of the heart. The pairing is natural: a tender-memory card meets a receptive-memory stone, and the meeting of the inner child becomes a little more likely to nourish the present rather than hide from it.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is the Six of Cups a yes or no card?

Upright, the Six of Cups is a tender, nostalgic **yes** — the card favours warmth, reunion, and the past offering a gift. Reversed, it leans **maybe**: the nostalgia has curdled into stuckness or is finally releasing; discern which.

### What does the Six of Cups mean in love?

In love, the Six of Cups signals tenderness, nostalgia, and often reunion — a reconnection with someone from the past, or a relationship whose warmth carries an earlier innocence. Reversed, watch for an idealised past connection refusing the present.

### Is the Six of Cups a bad card?

No. The Six of Cups is a tenderness card, not a bad omen. Even reversed it is not a curse — it simply observes that the nostalgia may be serving as refuge and invites honest reflection on whether it serves or hides from the present.

### What is the difference between Six of Cups and Six of Wands?

Both carry the number 6 — harmony / resolution — but their *flavour* differs entirely. The Six of Cups **turns tenderly toward the past** (the inner child, the reunion, the flowered cup); the Six of Wands **arrives crowned in public** (the laurel, the saluting crowd, the recognised victory). One holds a cup of flowers from a child; the other wears a wreath of laurel.

### Which crystal is best for Six of Cups?

**Moonstone** is the best overall crystal for the Six of Cups — its Water-element receptive intuition and inner-child work match the card's tender-memory archetype. For the daily inner-child tending as a living practice, wear Rhodochrosite.
