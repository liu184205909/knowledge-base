# The Letting Go Tarot Spread with Releasing Crystals

Letting go sounds simple until you try it. The cards don't make it easier by predicting whether you should; they make it workable by **showing you what you're actually holding, why you're holding it, what it's costing you, and how to release it without forcing.** That's the shape of this 4-card spread.

This is a spread for endings and releases — the close of a relationship, the end of a job, the lingering attachment to a version of yourself or a future that didn't arrive. The discipline is in card 2 (Why You're Holding It) — the card that names the real reason underneath the surface story. The crystals in this spread support the process rather than force the release, because genuine letting go can't be rushed.

## When to Use the Letting Go Spread

This is **a release-clarity spread**. Reach for it when:

- You're at the end of something — a relationship, a job, a phase, a hope — and you want to release cleanly rather than lingering.
- You keep returning to something you've officially "let go of" and want to understand why it still has a grip.
- You're holding onto something and you're not sure if it's attachment or genuine alignment.

**When to reach for something else:** for full-moon release rituals, the [Full Moon spread](/full-moon-spread/) includes release as part of a cycle. For deeper pattern work underneath an attachment, the [Shadow spread](/shadow-spread/). For deciding whether to stay or leave, the [Decision spread](/decision-spread/). The Letting Go spread assumes you've already decided to release — and helps you do it well.

**Important:** This spread is a self-awareness tool for ordinary release work, not a substitute for therapy or mental-health care. Grief, deep loss, and the aftermath of significant endings often need qualified support. The cards can prompt reflection; they can't do the integration work that real grieving requires.

## The 4 Positions of the Letting Go Spread

The layout is a descending line of four cards — read top to bottom, echoing the descent of release.

### Position 1 — What You're Holding

The **thing you're actually holding onto** — which is often different from what you'd name in passing. You might think you're holding onto a person; the card shows you're holding onto a story about who you were with them. You might think you're holding onto a job; the card shows you're holding onto an identity. This card names the real grip.

A **Major Arcana** card here suggests what you're holding carries significant psychological weight. The Devil often points to attachment; the Moon to a story or fantasy; the Tower to a structure ready to release.

### Position 2 — Why You're Holding It

The **real reason underneath the surface story** — and this is often the most useful card in the spread. We hold things for reasons: because they're familiar, because they once meant something, because releasing them means admitting something, because they protect us from a deeper fear. This card names the actual function the holding is serving.

Reversed cards here often suggest the real reason is partly hidden — give it time, don't force a clean answer.

### Position 3 — What It Costs You

The **cost of continuing to hold** — the energy, opportunity, presence, or growth that's being drained by the grip. This card often surfaces something you've been half-aware of: what you can't begin while you're still holding this. Read it honestly, because the cost is part of what motivates release.

### Position 4 — How to Release

The **path of release** — and importantly, this isn't usually a single dramatic act. Genuine release is a process, often a daily practice, and the card names the quality or specific move the release asks for. Sometimes it's a ritual (writing and burning a letter, a deliberate conversation, a physical clearing); sometimes it's an internal shift (accepting what was, softening a story, allowing grief to move). Reversed cards here often suggest the right pace is slow, and that release can't be forced.

## How to Read the Letting Go Spread

1. **Phrase the focus specifically.** "What do I need to understand about letting go of [the relationship / the job / the version of myself I thought I'd be by now]?" beats an undefined "what should I release?"
2. **Draw four cards in the descending line.**
3. **Read card 1 (What You're Holding) first and sit with it.** Often the surface framing isn't the real grip. Let the card reframe.
4. **Read card 2 (Why) longest.** This card often explains everything else. The real reason underneath the holding is the leverage point for release.
5. **Read card 3 (Cost) honestly.** What's being drained by continuing to hold?
6. **End on card 4 (How) with a real commitment.** What's one concrete act, ritual, or internal shift the release asks for? Name it specifically.
7. **Be patient with the process.** Genuine release often takes longer than a single reading. Return to the spread if the grip returns.

## An Eastern Lens on the Letting Go Spread

The spread maps onto the Eastern principle of **舍 (she, releasing/renouncing)** — not as loss, but as the deliberate practice of putting down what no longer serves growth. In both Buddhist and Daoist frames, holding and gripping are seen as the source of much suffering; 舍 is the antidote, and it's a practice, not a single act.

- **What You're Holding (1) is the 执 (zhi, the attachment)** — the specific grip, often subtler than it appears.
- **Why You're Holding It (2) is the 因 (yin, the cause) of the attachment** — usually a function the holding serves (protection, familiarity, identity, fear of what comes next).
- **What It Costs You (3) is the 苦 (ku, the suffering/cost) of the grip** — what's being lost or blocked by continuing to hold.
- **How to Release (4) is the practice of 舍** — the concrete discipline of putting down, gradually and gently.

This lens matters because it reframes letting go from a single act of will to a sustained practice. The cards don't tell you to "just let go" — they show you what you're holding, why, what it costs, and the path of release. The crystals support that practice — Smoky Quartz for the steady seeing of the grip, Labradorite for the illumination of the cause, Rose Quartz for compassion toward the cost, Moonstone for the gentle pace of genuine release. None of them "cut cords" or "release karma"; they support the steady, often gradual practice of putting down what's ready to be put down.

## Common Mistakes + Your Free Will

**Treating the spread as a verdict that you should let go.** The spread assumes you're considering release and helps you understand the holding. If the cards show that what you're holding actually still serves you, that's information too — not every grip is unhealthy, and not every release is necessary right now.

**Forcing release before its time.** Genuine release has its own pace. If Position 4 (How to Release) names a slow or gradual process, respect that. Forcing dramatic release often just pushes the attachment underground.

**Skipping card 2 (Why You're Holding It).** The why is the leverage point. If you try to release without understanding what function the holding serves, the grip tends to return in a different form. Sit with Position 2 longest.

**Doing letting-go readings instead of grieving.** Real loss — the death of someone, the end of a long relationship, the loss of a future you'd planned — often needs grief, not just release. If the cards surface heavy grief, that's a cue to work with a qualified therapist, not just a deck.

**Tarot spreads are a mirror for reflection, not a fixed forecast — the positions show energy, and you always have free will to choose your next step.** For the Letting Go spread, the cards may surface what you're holding, why, what it costs, and a path of release, but the actual release — the practice, the patience, the grief that may need to move — is entirely yours. The spread shows the field; the letting go is your work.

## FAQ

**What if card 1 shows I'm holding something I didn't realize?**
That's common — and often the most useful reading. The surface framing of what we're holding is rarely the real grip. Sit with the card and see what it's actually pointing at beneath the story you've been telling yourself.

**What if card 4 (How to Release) seems to suggest a slow process when I want it over?**
Release has its own pace. If the card names gradual work (a daily practice, a slow softening, a long process of acceptance), respect that. Forcing release before its time usually just buries the attachment.

**Is this the same as the Full Moon spread?**
They overlap but aren't the same. The [Full Moon spread](/full-moon-spread/) includes release as part of a monthly lunar cycle. The Letting Go spread is specifically for releasing a particular attachment — a relationship, a job, an identity — and goes deeper into the psychology of the grip.

**What if I keep needing this spread for the same thing?**
If the same grip returns after multiple letting-go readings, the work may be deeper than the spread can reach. The [Shadow spread](/shadow-spread/) might surface what's underneath, or a qualified therapist might help with the underlying pattern.
