As a volcanic glass, obsidian lacks an internal crystalline lattice and fractures conchoidally to produce razor-sharp edges. Ancient Mesoamerican artisans valued this property for surgical blades and ceremonial mirrors, while modern gemologists note its amorphous structure traps trace elements like titanium and magnetite. Many find its deep black surface useful for reflecting straightforward honesty.

Tradition frequently links its geometric precision to structured planning and calculated risk.
