<p>Citrine belongs to the quartz family and contains trace iron impurities that give it its pale yellow to honey color. Historically, merchants and scholars kept it near writing desks because its translucency made it easy to read by window light. When used in <strong>crystals for morning meditation</strong>, many people hold citrine to invite a steady focus. @@@AI_CRYSTAL_CARNELIAN@@@
<p>Carnelian is an opaque variety of chalcedony, typically ranging from orange to reddish-brown. Ancient artisans carved it into seals and amulets because the stone’s hardness (6.5–7 on the Mohs scale) resists scratching and wear. In traditional contexts, it was often associated with vitality and forward motion. For a morning session, hold carnelian in your right hand while placing your left hand over your lower abdomen. Breathe at a natural pace and notice the slight weight difference between the two sides. This asymmetrical grip encourages a grounded posture and keeps the mind engaged with physical sensation rather than abstract planning.</p>

Hold the stone between your thumb and middle finger, pressing lightly. Inhale for four counts, exhale for six, and visualize the mineral’s clarity mirroring your own awareness. <p>Selenite is a soft gypsum variety that forms in long, transparent prisms.
