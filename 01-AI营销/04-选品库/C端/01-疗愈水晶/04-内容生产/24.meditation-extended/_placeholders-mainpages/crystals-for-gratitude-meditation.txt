<p><strong>Citrine</strong> is a variety of silicon dioxide colored by iron impurities that produce yellow to orange tones. Its stable crystalline structure yields a reliable piezoelectric response, which some researchers note influences how the stone interacts with static charge. In the Theravada tradition, <em>gārava</em> (respect) and <em>kataññu</em> (gratitude) frame appreciation as a grounded ethical practice rather than a fleeting emotion. Placing citrine during a slow receiving breath invites a steady anchor for naming small, tangible wins. @@@AI_CRYSTAL_AMETHYST@@@
<p><strong>Amethyst</strong> is a quartz variety colored by iron and aluminum impurities that absorb longer visible wavelengths, leaving a violet spectrum. The mineral’s resistance to chemical weathering allows it to retain its structure even after prolonged handling. In the Tibetan tradition of <em>Dzogchen</em>, recognizing the natural stillness of mind aligns with pausing to notice quiet moments. Amethyst’s cooler tone pairs well with a slow receiving breath, inviting a deliberate inventory of unforced comforts rather than urgent demands. Many people find its visual depth supports a calm wind-down, keeping attention anchored to present-moment observation instead of future planning.</p>
