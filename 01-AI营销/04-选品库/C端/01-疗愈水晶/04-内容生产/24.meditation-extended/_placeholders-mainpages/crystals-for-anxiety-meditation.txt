<p>Amethyst belongs to the quartz family, composed primarily of silicon dioxide with trace iron impurities that produce its characteristic violet banding. Monastic communities across South and East Asia have long associated violet hues with quiet reflection, often placing polished specimens near altars to mark transition from active study to stillness. Many people find that pressing amethyst against the right palm grounds rapid thoughts, so pair the stone with the six-count exhale and notice how the cool weight steadies finger movement and encourages slower breathing.</p>
