@@@AI_CRYSTAL_MOONSTONE@@@
<p><strong>Moonstone</strong> belongs to the orthoclase feldspar group and exhibits adularescence, a soft glowing light caused by light scattering between alternating layers of two different feldspars. This optical effect occurs within a monoclinic crystal system. Traditionally, moonstone has been linked to lunar cycles and feminine rhythms, often worn to encourage emotional fluidity and seasonal awareness. For those aligned with the <strong>muluc mayan sign</strong>, moonstone offers a grounded way to track mood shifts against actual moon phases, turning abstract feelings into observable patterns that support calm decision-making.</p>

<p><strong>Aquamarine</strong> is a variety of beryl, chemically a beryllium-aluminum silicate, and ranks between 7.5 and 8 on the Mohs hardness scale. Its pale blue-to-green color comes from trace iron ions within its crystal lattice. In contemporary metaphysical practice, aquamarine is frequently associated with calming waters, mental clarity, and releasing stagnant energy.
