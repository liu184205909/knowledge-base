<p><strong>Carnelian</strong></p>
<p><em>Science:</em> Carnelian is a chalcedony variety of quartz, typically ranging from pale yellow to deep orange-red due to iron oxide impurities. With a hardness of 6.5 to 7 on the Mohs scale, it is durable enough for everyday wear and often displays natural banding patterns formed during slow crystallization.</p>
<p><em>Tradition:</em> The link between carnelian and the feline archetype belongs to modern crystal culture rather than ancient record. Contemporary practitioners associate the stone’s warm hues with forward motion and personal agency, creating a current-day pairing that aligns with themes of self-directed action.</p>
<p><em>Mindfulness Application:</em> Hold a polished carnelian piece while asking where you tend to over-explain yourself instead of simply acting. Use the stone as a tactile reminder to claim space for independence without guilt, allowing its steady presence to ground your next steps.</p>

<p><strong>Tiger Eye</strong></p>
<p><em>Science:</em> Tiger eye forms through a pseudomorphic replacement process where silica substitutes for crocidolite asbestos fibers. Its iron-rich composition produces characteristic golden to reddish-brown bands.</p>
<p><em>Tradition:</em> Historical texts do not document tiger eye in connection with feline symbolism. <p><strong>Onyx</strong></p>
<p><em>Science:</em> Onyx is a banded variety of chalcedony quartz, typically featuring parallel layers of white and black minerals deposited by silica-rich groundwater in volcanic rock cavities. Its consistent crystalline structure makes it highly polishable and resistant to daily wear.</p>
<p><em>Tradition:</em> Ancient civilizations used onyx for seals and cameos, but the stone’s connection to feline energy reflects a modern interpretation. Current wellness circles frequently pair onyx with themes of personal boundaries and quiet sovereignty, establishing a contemporary rather than historical link.</p>
<p><em>Mindfulness Application:</em> Press an onyx piece against your palm when feeling pressured to reveal too much.
