@@@AI_CRYSTAL_ONYX@@@
<p><strong>Onyx</strong> is a variety of chalcedony composed primarily of silicon dioxide, typically forming in parallel bands through the slow cooling of silica-rich fluids. In reflective practice, many use its dense, layered appearance to explore the concept of grounding before speaking, a quality that complements the crow’s emphasis on thoughtful communication. Holding onyx while reviewing recent conversations can help you identify where silence served you and where a clearer boundary might be needed. This modern pairing invites you to consider how steady presence supports honest expression, without making claims about supernatural effects.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_SODALITE@@@
<p><strong>Sodalite</strong> is an opaque feldspathoid mineral rich in sodium aluminum silicates, usually marked by white calcite veins and occasional pyrite flecks. Its consistent blue-white patterning offers a visual metaphor for sorting information, which aligns with the crow’s role as a messenger. When working with sodalite, many pause to map out recurring patterns in their daily interactions, asking which conversations require attention and which can be released. This stone serves as a mindful reminder that clarity often emerges from structured observation rather than impulsive reaction.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_LABRADORITE@@@
<p><strong>Labradorite</strong> is a plagioclase feldspar known for its labradorescence, an optical phenomenon caused by light scattering within alternating mineral layers formed during cooling. The stone’s shifting iridescence mirrors the crow’s capacity for adaptation and perspective shifts. Practitioners often use labradorite during moments of transition, reflecting on how changing viewpoints can reveal new solutions. By holding the stone while considering recent setbacks, you can explore which habits need adjustment and which messages remain worth carrying forward.</p>
