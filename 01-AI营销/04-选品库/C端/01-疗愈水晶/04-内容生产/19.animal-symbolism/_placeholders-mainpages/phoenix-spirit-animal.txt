Historically, various cultures have worn or carried this stone for comfort and vitality, though contemporary jewelers treat it as a modern association for creative momentum. <p><strong>Citrine</strong> forms when quartz crystals contain aluminum impurities and experience mild heat, resulting in a transparent to pale yellow gemstone valued for its durability and clarity. Its dense weight and rich color provide a grounding sensation against the skin, which some people find helpful for stabilizing nerves during periods of significant personal change or when establishing new routines.</p>
