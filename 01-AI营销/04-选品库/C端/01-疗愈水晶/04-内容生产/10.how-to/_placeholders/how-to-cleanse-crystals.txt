@@@AI_M2_PARA2_WHY_TRADITION@@@
<p>So why cleanse at all? Across many traditions, a new crystal is seen as carrying the handling of many hands — the miners, shippers, shopkeepers, and previous owners who passed it along before it reached you. Cleansing is the ritual of resetting a stone's symbolic energy state so the piece can begin fresh with you. Some people tie it to the lunar cycle (a full-moon reset), others to milestones — a new job, a move, a difficult week. The act itself is simple: a pause, a breath, a deliberate gesture of care. @@@AI_M5_INTRO@@@
<p>Most crystal damage doesn't come from a single dramatic accident — it comes from small, repeatable mistakes made in the name of cleansing. The six below are the ones we see again and again. Each one is easy to avoid once you know it, and each fix points back to the same principle: reach for a universal-safe method first, and confirm a stone's tolerance before reaching for water or salt.</p>

@@@AI_M5_MISTAKE_SELENITE_WATER@@@
<strong>Rinsing selenite under the tap.</strong> Selenite is a soft gypsum (Mohs 2) and can be damaged, scratched, or degraded by prolonged water exposure — a quick rinse may already be enough to dull its surface. <em>Fix:</em> keep selenite dry. Cleanse it with moonlight or sound, and let it do the cleansing of other stones as a plate instead.

@@@AI_M5_MISTAKE_AMETHYST_SUN@@@
<strong>Leaving amethyst or rose quartz in direct sun to "charge."</strong> Color-sensitive quartz can fade with prolonged direct sunlight, turning a rich purple or pink noticeably paler over time. <em>Fix:</em> use moonlight or indirect light instead — moonlight is reflected sunlight at roughly 1/400,000 the intensity, with no UV risk to any mineral.

@@@AI_M5_MISTAKE_SALT_METAL@@@
<strong>Using salt on a metal-chain bracelet.</strong> Salt is abrasive and accelerates oxidation in silver and copper, so a salt cleanse can tarnish a chain far faster than normal wear. <em>Fix:</em> for jewelry, skip salt entirely and rest the piece on a selenite plate overnight — same symbolic reset, zero metal damage.

@@@AI_M5_MISTAKE_BRACELET_SHOWER@@@
<strong>Wearing an elastic-cord bracelet in the shower to "cleanse" it.</strong> Hot water and soap degrade elastic cord (it loosens and eventually snaps) and leave a soapy film on the stones; metal settings can also oxidize. <em>Fix:</em> take the bracelet off and place it on a selenite plate or a moonlit windowsill — the no-contact methods were made for exactly this.

@@@AI_M5_MISTAKE_SALTWATER@@@
<strong>Using saltwater instead of dry salt.</strong> Saltwater combines the abrasiveness of salt with the soaking power of water, and it is not recommended for any specimen-quality crystal — it can etch soft stones, corrode metal, and leave residue in cracks. <em>Fix:</em> if you must use salt at all, use dry salt on a Mohs 7+ raw stone for a few hours only.

@@@AI_M5_MISTAKE_REUSING_SALT@@@
<strong>Reusing the same salt for multiple stones.</strong> In tradition, salt used for cleansing is discarded afterward — reusing it is said to redeposit what it drew out, and practically it accumulates moisture and grit. <em>Fix:</em> use a fresh bed of dry salt each time, then throw it away when you're done.

@@@AI_M6_SUMMARY@@@
<p>Most of this guide's jewelry advice is already woven through the method sections above, so think of this as a quick recap. The short version: <strong>for any bracelet, necklace, or pair of earrings, the no-contact methods are your default</strong>. A selenite plate, a moonlit windowsill, or a singing bowl cleanses the whole piece — stone, cord, and metal — without exposing any of it to water, salt, or handling. Save water and salt for loose, durable raw stones, and keep them away from anything with elastic cord or metal chain.</p>

@@@AI_M6_TIP_WHICH_HAND@@@
<p><strong>Which hand?</strong> In many traditions, the non-dominant (receiving) hand is suggested for wearing a cleansing-focused bracelet, since the dominant hand is seen as the giving side. Treat this as a personal ritual choice rather than a rule — what matters is that you notice and care for the piece.</p>

@@@AI_M6_TIP_FULLMOON_WINDOWSILL@@@
<p><strong>Full-moon windowsill.</strong> Once a month, lay your bracelets and necklaces on a windowsill under the full moon overnight (8+ hours). It's the lowest-effort, lowest-risk reset in crystal care — no contact, no chemistry, no fuss.</p>

@@@AI_M6_TIP_NEW_BRACELET_FIRST@@@
<p><strong>A new bracelet's first cleansing.</strong> When a new piece arrives, give it a selenite-plate or moonlight reset before its first wear — it marks the piece as yours and starts it from a clean slate. From there, a monthly full-moon cycle is usually enough for daily-wear pieces.</p>
