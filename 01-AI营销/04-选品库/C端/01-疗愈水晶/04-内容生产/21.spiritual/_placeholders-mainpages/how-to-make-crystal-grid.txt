@@@AI_CRYSTAL_CLEAR_QUARTZ@@@
<p><strong>Clear quartz</strong> consists of silicon dioxide with a well-defined hexagonal crystal lattice. Its piezoelectric properties—meaning it generates a small electrical charge under mechanical stress—are documented in materials science. In various contemplative traditions, this mineral has been traditionally associated with mental clarity and straightforward focus. Within a reflective practice, many find a mindful anchor in its transparent structure, which encourages you to examine your goals without adding unnecessary complexity. When placed at the center of a grid, it simply marks the primary reference point for your intention.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_AMETHYST@@@
<p><strong>Amethyst</strong> is a variety of quartz whose violet hue results from trace iron impurities and natural radiation exposure during formation. Historically, Buddhist practitioners have incorporated this stone into prayer beads to support steady attention, while Western monastic customs traditionally associated it with sobriety and measured reflection. As a supporting stone in a grid, it does not produce immediate changes; rather, it invites reflection on patience and emotional balance. The deep color provides a visual cue that encourages slower breathing and reduced mental chatter during routine check-ins.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_ROSE_QUARTZ@@@
<p><strong>Rose quartz</strong> derives its pale pink tone from microscopic inclusions of titanium, iron, or manganese within its microcrystalline structure. Several Asian philosophical frameworks traditionally link gentle colors to compassionate regard, and modern practitioners often pair this stone with self-compassion exercises. Placed within a geometric layout, it serves as a symbolic reminder to soften rigid expectations during personal review. Many find a mindful anchor in its subdued presence, using it to explore relationship dynamics or internal dialogue without forcing resolution.</p>

A six-stone circle following a basic geometric pattern holds the same structural purpose as a complex multi-point design. Focus first on defining your goal, selecting a central marker, and placing a few supporting stones that you find visually calming. Over time, you can expand the layout if desired, but simplicity remains fully valid for sustained reflection.</p>
