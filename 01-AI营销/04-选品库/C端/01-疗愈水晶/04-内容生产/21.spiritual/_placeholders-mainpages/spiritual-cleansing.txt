@@@AI_CRYSTAL_SELENITE@@@
<p><strong>Selenite</strong> consists of calcium sulfate dihydrate, placing it within the gypsum mineral family. It forms transparent crystals with a hardness of roughly 3.5 on the Mohs scale, making it soft enough to polish but delicate for daily wear. Historically, various reflective traditions have linked its pale translucence to clarity and quietude, though these connections remain symbolic rather than scientific. In contemporary mindfulness routines, many associate selenite with creating open, uncluttered spaces. Practitioners often pair it with heavier grounding stones to balance lightness with stability, using the combination as a visual cue for mental decluttering during <em>spiritual cleansing</em> sessions.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_BLACK_TOURMALINE@@@
<p><strong>Black tourmaline</strong> belongs to the complex silicate group, typically enriched with iron, magnesium, and aluminum. Its crystal lattice exhibits piezoelectric and pyroelectric qualities, meaning it generates small electrical charges when subjected to pressure or heat. Folk traditions across several regions historically carried dark tourmaline pieces as protective amulets, viewing their dense appearance as a barrier against unwanted influences. Modern mindfulness frameworks translate this into boundary-setting exercises. Many users place black tourmaline near entryways or desks, pairing it with softer stones like rose quartz or selenite to maintain equilibrium between protection and openness during reflective practices.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_CLEAR_QUARTZ@@@
<p><strong>Clear quartz</strong> is composed of silicon dioxide and crystallizes in the trigonal system. Like tourmaline, it displays reliable piezoelectric behavior, a property utilized extensively in modern electronics. Throughout recorded history, diverse contemplative traditions have treated translucent quartz as a marker for focus and mental ordering, though these links remain cultural rather than empirical. Today, practitioners often select clear quartz to anchor attention during <em>spiritual cleansing</em> routines. It is frequently arranged alongside amethyst for calm or black tourmaline for contrast, allowing the user to track shifts in mood or room atmosphere without relying on supernatural explanations.</p>
