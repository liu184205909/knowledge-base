@@@AI_CRYSTAL_RED_JASPER@@@
<p><strong>Red Jasper</strong> contains iron oxides and hematite inclusions that give it a dense, opaque red hue and a Mohs hardness of approximately 6.5. In indigenous Australian and Celtic lore, this stone was carried to support endurance during long travel. During practice, hold it against your lower abdomen while focusing on your breath; the cool, heavy weight serves as a <em>gentle companion</em> for returning to stillness when thoughts drift.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_CARNELIAN@@@
<p><strong>Carnelian</strong> is a cryptocrystalline form of silica colored by trace iron impurities, typically forming in basalt cavities. Classical Sanskrit texts associate it with <em>vitality and creative motion</em>. When placed near the navel region during your session, use the stone’s warm tone to track your exhalations; many practitioners report that this simple pairing encourages a relaxed awareness of bodily sensations without forcing change.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_CITRINE@@@
<p><strong>Citrine</strong> consists of silicon dioxide with iron impurities that produce its characteristic yellow to orange spectrum, and it remains chemically stable under normal handling conditions. Historically, merchants kept it in strongboxes as a symbol of clear judgment and steady resolve. Position it above the navel while you maintain an upright spine; the stone’s consistent surface provides a <em>mindful anchor</em> for noticing how posture influences breath depth.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_ROSE_QUARTZ@@@
<p><strong>Rose Quartz</strong> is an aluminum silicate mineral whose pale pink color stems from trace amounts of titanium, iron, or manganese. Buddhist and Hindu lineages often link soft hues with <em>metta</em> or compassionate attention. Rest it over the center of your chest during the heart segment of your sequence; the stone’s smooth texture invites slower breathing and a quieter internal dialogue.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_SODALITE@@@
<p><strong>Sodalite</strong> is a sodium aluminum silicate chloride mineral known for its deep blue veining and conchoidal fracture pattern. Traditional Chinese medicinal texts occasionally referenced similar blue stones for clearing heat, though modern usage focuses on its visual steadiness. Place it along the throat area while you concentrate on the space between your shoulders; the stone’s matte finish helps many people observe their inner voice without rushing to fill silence.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_AMETHYST@@@
<p><strong>Amethyst</strong> forms when pure quartz incorporates iron impurities and undergoes natural irradiation, yielding its signature violet bands and a hardness of seven on the Mohs scale. In the <em>Tibetan tradition of Dzogchen</em>, deep purple tones are sometimes used in visualizations to support clear seeing. Hold it near your brow center during the third-eye phase; the cool surface reminds you to watch passing thoughts as temporary weather rather than fixed truths.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_CLEAR_QUARTZ@@@
<p><strong>Clear Quartz</strong> is composed almost entirely of silicon dioxide and exhibits piezoelectric properties when pressure is applied to its crystal lattice. Across multiple Asian lineages, transparent stones are associated with <em>open awareness</em> and unclouded perception. Position it at the crown of your head while you allow your attention to widen; the stone’s lack of pigment supports a simple wind-down routine where you rest in quiet observation without directing focus.</p>
