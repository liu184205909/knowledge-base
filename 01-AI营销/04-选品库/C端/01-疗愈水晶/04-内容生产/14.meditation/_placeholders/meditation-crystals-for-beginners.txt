@@@AI_M3_CRYSTAL_TRANSITION@@@
<p>Selecting the right stone depends on your personal preference and the specific quality you wish to explore. Below are six accessible options, each offering unique physical and traditional characteristics for your practice.</p>

@@@AI_M3_CLEAR_QUARTZ_THREE_ELEMENT@@@
<p><strong>Clear Quartz</strong> is silicon dioxide (SiO₂), rated Mohs 7, and exhibits piezoelectric properties used in electronics. Traditionally described as a 'master' stone, it is placed anywhere on the body to amplify intent. Mindfully, its transparent clarity offers a neutral visual focal point that does not compete with internal imagery.</p>

@@@AI_M3_AMETHYST_THREE_ELEMENT@@@
<p><strong>Amethyst</strong> is a purple quartz variety where color arises from natural irradiation of iron impurities. Long associated with clarity, it is traditionally placed near the crown or third eye. Holding its cool, smooth surface during breathwork gives the mind a single, steady point of contact.</p>

@@@AI_M3_ROSE_QUARTZ_THREE_ELEMENT@@@
<p><strong>Rose Quartz</strong> is a pink quartz variety colored by titanium, manganese, and irradiation, rated Mohs 7. It is widely associated with gentleness and the heart center across many cultures. Its smooth, curved shape fits naturally in the palm, acting as a tactile 'worry stone' for emotional release.</p>

@@@AI_M3_SELENITE_THREE_ELEMENT@@@
<p><strong>Selenite</strong> is calcium sulfate dihydrate (CaSO₄·2H₂O), a soft Mohs 2 mineral that is fibrous and light-transmitting. Many traditions describe it as self-cleansing, often using it as a plate to rest other stones. As a mindfulness tool, a flat selenite piece becomes a designated 'resting place' between sessions.</p>

@@@AI_M3_SMOKY_QUARTZ_THREE_ELEMENT@@@
<p><strong>Smoky Quartz</strong> is a translucent brown-grey quartz variety whose stable color comes from natural irradiation. It is traditionally associated with grounding and the release of heavy emotions. Its substantial weight and soft tone pair well with visualizations of stability and calm.</p>

@@@AI_M3_HOWLITE_THREE_ELEMENT@@@
<p><strong>Howlite</strong> is a white borate mineral with grey veining, rated Mohs 3.5 and naturally porous. Modern traditions often link it to calming the mind for sleep and bedtime rituals. Tucking a soft howlite under the pillow can serve as a gentle cue for settling into rest.</p>

@@@AI_M4_INTRO@@@
<p>This five-step sequence is a simple starting practice designed to be executable immediately. It requires no prior experience, only a quiet space and one chosen stone.</p>

@@@AI_M4_STEP_1_DEEPEN@@@
<p><strong>Find a quiet seat and breathe naturally.</strong> Do not force deep breaths; just notice ten natural breaths, silently counting each cycle. Allow your shoulders to drop and your jaw to unclench as you settle into the chair.</p>

@@@AI_M4_STEP_2_DEEPEN@@@
<p><strong>Hold one stone in the left palm.</strong> Choose only one stone. Feel its weight, temperature, and surface texture against your skin. Let your fingers rest loosely around it, creating a gentle connection without gripping tightly.</p>

@@@AI_M4_STEP_3_DEEPEN@@@
<p><strong>Rest your attention on the stone.</strong> When the mind wanders, gently return to the feeling of the stone in your hand. That return is the whole practice. There is no need to analyze the sensation, only to acknowledge it.</p>

@@@AI_M4_STEP_4_DEEPEN@@@
<p><strong>Picture a small warm sphere of light.</strong> Visualize a simple, warm sphere of soft light resting in your palm alongside the stone. Keep the image basic and unelaborated. This serves as a secondary anchor if the tactile sensation becomes faint.</p>

@@@AI_M4_STEP_5_DEEPEN@@@
<p><strong>Set a simple intention.</strong> Silently say, "I begin, simply." One short line is enough to frame your session. This affirmation grounds your purpose without adding complexity to the mental landscape.</p>

@@@AI_M4_STEP_6_DEEPEN@@@
<p><strong>Close with a small smile.</strong> Lift the corners of your mouth gently, signaling comfort to your nervous system. Open your eyes slowly, take one final deep breath, and set the stone down on a nearby surface.</p>

@@@AI_M4_CLOSING_NOTE@@@
<p>This routine takes only 5-10 minutes. Consistency matters more than duration. By repeating this simple path, you build a reliable habit of returning to the present moment.</p>
