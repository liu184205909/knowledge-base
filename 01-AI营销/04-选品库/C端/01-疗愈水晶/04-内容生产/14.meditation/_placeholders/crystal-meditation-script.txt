@@@AI_M3_CLEAR_QUARTZ_THREE_ELEMENT@@@
Clear Quartz is composed of silicon dioxide (SiO₂) and ranks 7 on the Mohs scale, making it durable and piezoelectric, a property utilized in electronics. Traditionally described across cultures as a 'master' stone, it has been used for centuries to support mental clarity and amplify intention. As a mindfulness tool, its transparent clarity serves as a neutral visual object that does not compete with visualization, allowing the mind to focus purely on the breath.

@@@AI_M3_AMETHYST_THREE_ELEMENT@@@
Amethyst is a violet variety of quartz, also SiO₂, with its color derived from natural irradiation of iron impurities, ranking 7 on the Mohs scale. It has a long history of association with clarity and stillness, often placed near the crown or third eye in meditative traditions to promote calm. The cool, smooth surface of the stone provides a tactile anchor, helping the mind settle by focusing on the contrast between the stone's temperature and the breath.

@@@AI_M3_ROSE_QUARTZ_THREE_ELEMENT@@@
Rose Quartz is a pink variety of quartz, colored by trace amounts of titanium or manganese and irradiation, with a hardness of 7 on the Mohs scale. Culturally, it is widely recognized as a symbol of gentleness and unconditional love, frequently associated with the heart center in yoga and meditation practices. Its soft pink hue and smooth, rounded shape make it a natural 'worry stone,' fitting comfortably in the palm to provide a soothing sensory experience during emotional processing.

@@@AI_M3_BLACK_TOURMALINE_THREE_ELEMENT@@@
Black Tourmaline, or Schorl, is a complex borosilicate mineral with the formula NaFe³⁺₃Al₆(BO₃)₃Si₆O₁₈(OH)₄, ranking 7–7.5 on the Mohs scale and exhibiting pyroelectric properties. It is traditionally valued in both Western and Eastern practices as a grounding stone, believed to offer protection and stability through its dense, dark presence. The noticeable weight and density of the stone in the hand serve as a powerful tactile anchor, physically pulling awareness down into the body during grounding exercises.

@@@AI_M3_CITRINE_THREE_ELEMENT@@@
Citrine is a yellow to orange variety of quartz, colored by iron and heat treatment, with a durable hardness of 7 on the Mohs scale. Historically linked to warmth, sunlight, and abundance, it has been used in various traditions to inspire positivity and mental clarity. As a mindfulness aid, its warm-toned appearance and smooth texture act as a tactile cue for positive intention, helping to shift focus toward resolve and optimism during meditation.
