@@@AI_M3_AMETHYST_THREE_ELEMENT@@@
<p>Amethyst is a purple variety of quartz (<em>SiO₂</em>), where color arises from natural irradiation interacting with iron impurities, making it a durable Mohs 7 stone that cools quickly against the skin. Traditionally, it has been linked to clarity and stillness, often placed near the crown or third-eye area to encourage mental silence. Holding a cool amethyst bead during breathwork provides a singular tactile anchor, helping to quiet internal chatter by focusing attention on the sensation of cold.</p>

@@@AI_M3_CLEAR_QUARTZ_THREE_ELEMENT@@@
<p>Clear quartz is silicon dioxide (<em>SiO₂</em>) with a Mohs hardness of 7, known for its piezoelectric properties and transparent clarity that serves as a neutral visual field. Across many cultures, it is viewed as a "master" stone, versatile enough to be placed anywhere on the body to amplify intention without imposing a specific hue. Its colorless nature makes it an ideal focal point for visualization, allowing the mind to project images without visual competition from the stone itself.</p>

@@@AI_M3_FLUORITE_THREE_ELEMENT@@@
<p>Fluorite (<em>CaF₂</em>) is a relatively soft Mohs 4 mineral famous for its octahedral cleavage and vibrant, layered color bands that organize visual attention. Modern traditions associate it with mental structure and order, suggesting its geometric patterns help sort through scattered thoughts. The distinct stripes within the stone offer a visual metaphor for organizing chaos, guiding the eye and mind toward a sense of systematic calm during complex problem-solving meditations.</p>

@@@AI_M3_ROSE_QUARTZ_THREE_ELEMENT@@@
<p>Rose quartz is a pink variety of quartz (<em>SiO₂</em>), colored by trace titanium and manganese, with a Mohs hardness of 7 that feels smooth and warm in the palm. It is widely associated with gentleness and the heart center, offering a soft visual tone that invites self-compassion. Its rounded, palm-friendly shape makes it a natural "worry stone," allowing you to rub its surface while practicing affirmations of kindness to reduce tension.</p>

@@@AI_M3_SELENITE_THREE_ELEMENT@@@
<p>Selenite is a variety of gypsum (<em>CaSO₄·2H₂O</em>), very soft at Mohs 2, with a fibrous structure that transmits light and can be damaged by water. In many traditions, it is considered self-cleansing and is often used as a plate to rest other stones, symbolizing purity and clear channeling. As a long wand or flat plate, it serves as a designated "resting place" between meditation sessions, helping to mark the transition from practice to daily life.</p>

@@@AI_M3_BLACK_TOURMALINE_THREE_ELEMENT@@@
<p>Black tourmaline (schorl) is a complex borosilicate mineral with a Mohs hardness of 7–7.5, notable for being pyroelectric and dense, giving it a substantial weight in the hand. It is traditionally used for grounding and protection, believed to help anchor the practitioner firmly to the earth. The noticeable heaviness of the stone provides a strong tactile cue for grounding practices, pulling scattered energy downward and stabilizing the mind.</p>

@@@AI_M3_SMOKY_QUARTZ_THREE_ELEMENT@@@
<p>Smoky quartz is a brown-grey variety of quartz (<em>SiO₂</em>), colored by natural irradiation, and shares the durable Mohs 7 hardness of its clear counterpart. It is often associated with releasing emotional burdens and grounding, serving as a stabilizing presence during intense meditation. Its translucent, muted tone pairs visual softness with grounding weight, making it an excellent choice for evening practices aimed at unwinding and letting go of the day's stress.</p>

@@@AI_M3_HOWLITE_THREE_ELEMENT@@@
<p>Howlite is a soft Mohs 3.5 borate mineral (<em>Ca₂B₅SiO₉(OH)₅</em>), naturally white with grey veining and porous, often dyed for jewelry to enhance its appearance. Modern traditions link it to calm and quieting the mind, particularly for bedtime routines. Its soft, pale appearance is frequently used in wind-down rituals, tucked under a pillow or held gently to signal to the body that it is time to settle into rest.</p>

@@@AI_M3_MOONSTONE_THREE_ELEMENT@@@
<p>Moonstone is a feldspar variety (<em>KAlSi₃O₈</em>) with a Mohs hardness of 6–6.5, displaying adularescence—a floating light sheen caused by its layered internal structure. It is deeply associated with lunar cycles, intuition, and new beginnings in many global traditions. The moving sheen of the stone gives the eye a soft, non-fixed point of rest, encouraging a dreamlike state of awareness that supports intuitive and reflective meditation practices.</p>

@@@AI_M3_CITRINE_THREE_ELEMENT@@@
<p>Citrine is a yellow-to-orange quartz variety (<em>SiO₂</em>), colored by iron and heat, with a durable Mohs 7 hardness that retains warmth well. Historically linked to sunlight and abundance, it is often used to invoke positivity and resolve. Its warm-toned tactile quality serves as a comforting cue during morning meditations, helping to set a bright intention for the day ahead without overstimulating the mind.</p>

@@@AI_M3_HEMATITE_THREE_ELEMENT@@@
<p>Hematite is an iron oxide mineral (<em>Fe₂O₃</em>) with a Mohs hardness of 5.5–6.5, distinctively heavy due to its high specific gravity and metallic grey-black finish. It is traditionally associated with grounding and stability, linking the practitioner to the earth through its dense mass. The unusual weight immediately draws attention downward, acting as a natural grounding cue that pulls focus away from racing thoughts and into the body.</p>

@@@AI_M3_LABRADORITE_THREE_ELEMENT@@@
<p>Labradorite is a plagioclase feldspar (<em>(Ca,Na)(Al,Si)₄O₈</em>) with a Mohs hardness of 6–6.5, known for labradorescence—flashes of blue and gold light from internal layering. It is often associated with intuition and inner sight, used to explore deeper layers of consciousness. The unexpected flashes of light provide a gentle point of surprise for the mind, helping to break repetitive thought patterns and invite fresh perspective during introspection.</p>

@@@AI_M3_LEPIDOLITE_THREE_ELEMENT@@@
<p>Lepidolite is a lithium-bearing mica mineral (<em>K(Li,Al)₃(Al,Si,Rb)₄O₁₀(F,OH)₂</em>) that is very soft at Mohs 2.5–3, with a flaky structure and lilac to grey-purple hues. In modern traditions, it is valued for promoting a calm, settled state, often used during wind-down periods. Its soft, cool texture is soothing to hold, making it a gentle companion for settling practices before sleep or after stressful events.</p>
