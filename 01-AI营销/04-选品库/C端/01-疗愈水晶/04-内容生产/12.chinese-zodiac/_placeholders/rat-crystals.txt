@@@AI_M6_DEEPEN@@@
In Five Elements theory, the five elements (Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water) interact in generating cycles (one feeds the next) and controlling cycles (one restrains the next). Water and Fire are direct counterparts — water cools fire, fire evaporates water — which means they can balance each other in small amounts but destabilize each other in large amounts. For a Water-element Rat, this is the logic behind the guideline to use large Fire-element stacks (Ruby, Carnelian, Sunstone in big amounts) mindfully. It doesn't mean a Rat can never wear Ruby; it means the tradition suggests Water-supporting stones (Aquamarine, Clear Quartz, Moonstone) as the dominant tone, with Fire stones as small accent pieces rather than the main stack. A single Garnet — which carries a warming quality without being a pure Fire stone — is generally read as a fine way to bring warming energy without over-stacking Fire.
