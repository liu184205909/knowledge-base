@@@AI_M6_DEEPEN@@@
In Five Elements theory, the five elements (Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water) interact in generating cycles (one feeds the next) and controlling cycles (one restrains the next). Water and Fire are direct counterparts — water cools fire, fire evaporates water — which means they can balance each other in small amounts but destabilize each other in large amounts. For a Water-element Pig, this is the logic behind the guideline to use large Fire-element stacks (Ruby, Carnelian, Sunstone in big amounts) mindfully. There's a useful nuance for Pigs specifically: a single signature Fire stone like Ruby (the Pig's lucky stone, worn for resonance) is generally read as fine; the guideline is about large dominant stacks that could over-stimulate the Water constitution. It doesn't mean a Pig can't wear their own lucky stone; it means the tradition suggests not stacking multiple large Fire stones together as the dominant wear.
