@@@AI_M6_DEEPEN@@@
In Five Elements theory, the five elements (Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water) interact in generating cycles (one feeds the next) and controlling cycles (one restrains the next). Fire controls Metal the way a furnace melts metal ore — useful in small amounts (it's how metal is shaped and refined) but potentially destructive in large amounts (it can melt the Metal entirely). For a Metal-element Monkey, this is the logic behind the guideline to use large Fire-element stacks (Ruby, Carnelian, Red Jasper in big amounts) mindfully. It doesn't mean a Monkey can never wear a red stone; it means the tradition suggests Metal-supporting stones (Citrine with its Earth-tone, Clear Quartz, Hematite) as the dominant tone, with Fire stones as small accent pieces rather than the main stack.
