@@@AI_CRYSTAL_BLACK_TOURMALINE@@@
<p>Formed as a complex silicate mineral, black tourmaline carries a dense crystalline lattice that provides a reliable physical anchor for threshold spaces. In the BTB tradition, this stone aligns with the Water element and is typically positioned at the main entry to help define the transition between public and private zones. Setting a polished piece on an entry console or near a doorway helps create a quiet visual boundary that many people find useful for maintaining personal space during daily routines.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_OBSIDIAN@@@
<p>This naturally occurring volcanic glass forms when lava cools rapidly, resulting in a smooth, conchoidal surface that reflects light with a focused intensity. Feng shui tradition places obsidian in the front-right Helpful People area, where its dense composition supports deep grounding during social interactions or collaborative projects. Keeping a tumbled stone on a nearby shelf or hallway stand serves as a subtle reminder to maintain clear boundaries while remaining open to connection.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_HEMATITE@@@
<p>Composed primarily of iron oxide, hematite possesses a distinctive metallic sheen and a notably heavy density that makes it physically reassuring to handle. Classical feng shui associates this stone with the Metal element, making it a natural fit for workspaces where electronic devices generate scattered attention. Placing a flat hematite slab beneath a desk or beside a computer monitor helps establish a stable focal point during long hours of focused work.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_SMOKY_QUARTZ@@@
<p>Smoky quartz develops through the slow crystallization of silica combined with trace aluminum and natural irradiation, giving it a translucent brown-gray hue that softens harsh lighting. Traditional practice recommends positioning this stone in rooms that consistently feel crowded or emotionally heavy, where its gentle molecular structure encourages a steady release of accumulated tension. Refreshing its placement each month by wiping the surface and moving it to a new corner helps maintain a consistent rhythm of space renewal.</p>
