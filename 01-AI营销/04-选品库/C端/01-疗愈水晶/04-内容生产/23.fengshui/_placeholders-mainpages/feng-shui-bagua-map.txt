@@@AI_CRYSTAL_AMETHYST@@@
<p>Amethyst is a violet variety of quartz that forms in volcanic cavities over millennia, its rich hue traditionally associated with the Wealth sector in the BTB framework. Positioned in the back-left corner of a room, it supports the area’s wood element and purple resonance without demanding active effort. In modern practice, keeping a small tumbled piece near a journal or desk invites a quiet moment of reflection before starting the day.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_CITRINE@@@
<p>Citrine naturally crystallizes in igneous rock formations, displaying a warm yellow to golden transparency that classical practitioners link to the Fire element. It belongs in the Fame zone at the back-center of a space, where its bright tones are believed to support recognition and personal expression. Setting a polished stone on a windowsill allows natural light to catch its facets, creating a simple visual cue for focusing on creative goals.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_ROSE_QUARTZ@@@
<p>Rose quartz develops through slow cooling magma cycles, yielding a soft pink transparency that the five elements connect to the Earth energy in the Love and Relationships corner. Placed in the back-right area of a bedroom or living space, it aligns with traditional pink tones and supports a sense of calm interaction. Many individuals keep a smooth specimen on a nightstand to encourage unhurried conversation and present-moment awareness.</p>
