@@@AI_CRYSTAL_CITRINE@@@
<p>Citrine is a variety of silicon dioxide colored by trace iron impurities, a composition that gives it a pale to deep golden transparency. In <strong>BTB practice</strong>, its Earth-element quality makes it well-suited for the Wealth bagua area, where it is often paired with amethyst to ground financial intentions without relying on color matching. Keeping a small tumbled piece near a cash box or home office desk supports a steady, unobtrusive focus for daily planning.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_PYRITE@@@
<p>Pyrite consists of iron sulfide arranged in a rigid cubic lattice, producing its characteristic metallic luster and sharp geometric faces. Within classical and BTB frameworks, its Metal element aligns with the Children and Creativity bagua sector, typically located in the northwest or far-right corner depending on the alignment method used. Placing a polished pyrite specimen near a workspace can encourage structured thinking and clear decision-making during project development.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_AVENTURINE@@@
<p>Aventurine is a quartz variety containing microscopic fuchsite platelets, which scatter light to create its soft green shimmer and slightly translucent finish. Traditional practice often links its Wood element to the Family and Growth areas, though some schools associate green with the Wealth corner; in either case, it is used to support gentle progression rather than sudden change. A smooth aventurine stone placed on a bedside table or meditation cushion can provide a quiet visual anchor for restorative routines.</p>
