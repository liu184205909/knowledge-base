@@@AI_CRYSTAL_CITRINE@@@
<p><strong>Citrine</strong> is a natural variety of quartz whose pale yellow to golden hue develops from trace iron impurities, typically forming after amethyst undergoes gentle geothermal heating. In classical feng shui, the stone aligns with the Earth element and is traditionally arranged in the Wealth sector—the back-left corner when standing at your entrance—to support a grounded approach to resources. Many people keep polished citrine points near home ledgers or workspace shelves, using the mineral’s warm tone as a quiet daily cue for intentional budgeting and long-term planning.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_BLACK_TOURMALINE@@@
<p><strong>Black Tourmaline</strong> is a complex silicate mineral that crystallizes slowly in volcanic rock, developing its dense, opaque structure through trace metallic elements like iron and magnesium. Traditional practice associates the stone with protective grounding, and many position it near doorways or in the Knowledge sector to support a sense of clear, settled energy during periods of transition. In modern mindful applications, a raw or tumbled piece on a nightstand or entry console simply serves as a tactile reminder to pause before reacting to outside pressures.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_ROSE_QUARTZ@@@
<p><strong>Rose Quartz</strong> is a delicate pink variety of quartz that develops its soft coloration from trace amounts of titanium, iron, or manganese during its formation in granite-rich environments. In the BTB tradition, the stone corresponds to the Earth element and is commonly placed in the Love area—the back-right corner when facing inward—to encourage steady, compassionate connections within the home. Rather than functioning as a remedy for relationship dynamics, many keep a small cluster on a dresser or reading chair to quietly reinforce the intention of patience and mutual respect.</p>

@@@AI_FAQ_ANSWER_1@@@
<p>No single stone ranks universally, because effectiveness depends on aligning the crystal’s traditional quality with your specific bagua target. Citrine supports resource awareness in the Wealth sector, black tourmaline grounds protective intention near entryways, and rose quartz encourages steady compassion in the Love area. The most practical approach is to choose one stone that matches the element and life focus you want to prioritize.</p>

@@@AI_FAQ_ANSWER_2@@@
<p>Traditional practice recommends placing one to three stones per sector, with the BTB lineage specifically favoring a cluster of three to represent heaven, earth, and humanity. Fewer than three keeps the arrangement visually quiet, while more than three can compete for attention and dilute the intended focus. Stick to a single cohesive group per bagua area to maintain clarity.</p>

@@@AI_FAQ_ANSWER_3@@@
<p>Cleansing is considered an important first step before any initial placement, though maintenance follows a simpler rhythm. Start by clearing the stones with moonlight, sound, or smoke, then return to dust and reset the arrangement monthly to keep the layout feeling deliberate. This routine preserves the stone’s role as a mindful anchor rather than allowing it to become background decor.</p>

@@@AI_FAQ_ANSWER_4@@@
<p>You can reuse a stone across different sectors, provided its traditional element and meaning align with each location’s purpose. However, duplicating the same crystal in competing areas often weakens the visual and intentional clarity of both placements. It usually works better to assign each piece to its strongest bagua match and reserve multiples only for dedicated clusters.</p>
