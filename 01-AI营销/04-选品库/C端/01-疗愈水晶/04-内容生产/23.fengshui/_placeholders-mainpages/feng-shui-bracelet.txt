In the BTB tradition, practitioners align bracelets with the front-door bagua area, placing the door at the bottom row to map Knowledge, Career, and Helpful People zones without a compass. In classical Eight Mansions feng shui, orientation relies on actual compass bearings, positioning Career toward North and Fame toward South, while the Ba Zhai system calculates four auspicious directions (Sheng Chi, Tien Yi, Nien Yen, Fu Wei) alongside four inauspicious ones, noting that the lunar year begins at Li Chun around February 4. The five elements describe how materials interact with space: Water aligns with black tones in the North sector for Career and Helpful People, Wood governs green hues in the Family zone, Fire corresponds to red in the Fame area, Earth anchors yellow in the Center, Knowledge, and Love sectors, and Metal relates to white in the Children and Helpful People regions. Based on tradition, wearing a stone this way is a mindfulness anchor, not a promise of prosperity, and the practice remains a traditional method for reflection rather than a guarantee.</p>

In traditional placement, many people wear these beads on the non-dominant wrist to invite abundance, aligning with the fire element’s upward-moving quality to support forward momentum. @@@AI_CRYSTAL_BLACK_TOURMALINE@@@
<p>Black tourmaline crystallizes as a complex borosilicate mineral, typically forming elongated prismatic structures that carry a dense, grounding presence. Based on traditional placement, wearing these stones on the dominant wrist helps project outward energy, which many wearers use to support a sense of clear, settled boundaries. Placing this bracelet near your workstation or entryway offers a tactile reference point for maintaining personal space during demanding routines.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_AMETHYST@@@
<p>Amethyst is a violet variety of quartz whose coloring comes from trace iron and aluminum combined with natural irradiation over time. In the BTB tradition, the signature purple shade aligns with the wealth corner of the bagua, inviting abundance through its steady, reflective frequency rather than direct financial promises. Many wearers keep an amethyst bracelet on their non-dominant wrist as a mindful cue to pause and reassess priorities during busy periods.</p>
