@@@AI_CRYSTAL_CITRINE@@@
<p><strong>Citrine</strong> forms from silicon dioxide where trace iron impurities create its characteristic golden hue, giving the stone a warm, translucent quality that catches light easily. In traditional layout practice, positioning this stone near the main entry or on a central coffee table aligns with the earth element’s grounding nature, inviting a sense of openness when guests arrive. You can treat the placement as a mindful daily cue to keep walkways clear and maintain eye contact with the doorway, reinforcing a welcoming rhythm without overcomplicating the room’s flow.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_QUARTZ@@@
<p><strong>Clear quartz</strong> consists of a tightly packed hexagonal lattice of silicon dioxide, a structure that produces its sharp transparency and consistent refractive index. Setting a clustered formation on a side table near the room’s core mirrors the classical emphasis on keeping the Tai Chi area unobstructed, which helps visual lines travel freely across the space. Many residents use the stone’s presence as a practical prompt to clear surface clutter regularly, turning a simple tidying habit into a quiet exercise in mental clarity.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_AVENTURINE@@@
<p><strong>Aventurine</strong> is a dense variety of feldspathic quartzite whose green tint comes from microscopic mica and copper platelets trapped during crystallization. Placing a smooth piece in the middle-left section of the room corresponds to the family sector in the BTB bagua grid, where the wood element traditionally governs growth and relational stability. You might arrange the stone on a low shelf to serve as a gentle reminder to check in with household routines, using its steady texture to anchor conversations around shared responsibilities and mutual support.</p>
