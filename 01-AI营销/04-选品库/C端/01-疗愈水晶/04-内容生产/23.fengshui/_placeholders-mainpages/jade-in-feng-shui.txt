@@@AI_CRYSTAL_JADE@@@
<p>Formed over millions of years through intense heat and pressure, <strong>jade</strong> consists of tightly interlocked calcium-magnesium or sodium-aluminum silicate crystals that give it remarkable toughness and a smooth, cool feel. In feng shui, its steady green resonance aligns with the Wood element and the Family area, making it a thoughtful addition to shared living spaces or family photo displays. Wearing a jade piece or keeping a polished tumbler on a wooden shelf can serve as a quiet reminder to prioritize patience and relational connection during busy seasons.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_AVENTURINE@@@
<p><strong>Aventurine</strong> is a variety of quartz containing microscopic platelets of mica or chlorite that catch the light and produce its characteristic shimmer. Traditionally grouped with jade under the Wood element, it supports the same growth-oriented frequency and is often arranged near plants or reading corners to encourage steady focus. Many people keep a small piece nearby when planning household routines, finding that its subtle visual warmth helps maintain a calm, forward-looking mindset.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_MOSS_AGATE@@@
<p><strong>Moss agate</strong> belongs to the chalcedony family and features delicate iron oxide or manganese dendrites that create natural patterns resembling lichen or forest floors. Its earthy green tones reinforce the Wood element’s connection to rootedness, making it well-suited for entryway consoles or meditation spaces where you want to establish clear boundaries. Placing it beside other green stones or fresh foliage can help anchor daily transitions and support a sense of settled energy throughout the home.</p>
