@@@AI_CRYSTAL_CARNELIAN@@@
<p>Carnelian is an opaque to translucent chalcedony variety of quartz, typically colored orange to reddish-brown by iron oxide impurities formed during volcanic or hydrothermal processes. In modern crystal traditions, it is often associated with sustained energy and courageous action, qualities that align well with the <strong>kenaz rune</strong>’s emphasis on active creation. Mindfully, many use a small piece of carnelian on their desk to mark the transition from planning to doing, letting its warm tone remind them to apply measured effort rather than rushed force.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_CITRINE@@@
<p>Citrine is a transparent to translucent variety of quartz, ranging from pale yellow to deep amber, often formed when rock is exposed to geothermal heat or natural sunlight over long periods. Contemporary wellness circles frequently link citrine to mental clarity and purposeful direction, echoing the illuminating aspect of the <strong>kenaz rune</strong>. As a mindfulness tool, practitioners may place citrine near reading materials or drafting tools to encourage focused observation, allowing the stone’s steady color to ground attention when reviewing complex details or refining a craft.</p>

@@@AI_CRYSTAL_TIGERS_EYE@@@
<p>Tiger’s eye is a chatoyant pseudomorph, originally crocidolite asbestos that has been replaced by silica and iron oxides, creating its characteristic golden-brown bands and silky luster. Modern interpretations often associate it with grounded confidence and clear boundaries, resonating with the controlled-fire theme of the <strong>kenaz rune</strong>. In daily practice, many keep tiger’s eye nearby while learning a new technique or troubleshooting a project, using its visual steadiness to support deliberate pacing and consistent effort over sudden bursts of activity.</p>
