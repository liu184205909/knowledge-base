What Is a Chakra Test?

A chakra test is a self-reflection quiz that helps identify which of your seven energy centers may be blocked or out of balance. By answering honest questions about how you currently feel across different areas of life, the test points to where your energy may be stuck — and the crystals traditionally used to support that chakra. It is a reflective tool for awareness, not a medical or psychological diagnosis.

Why Do Chakras Get Blocked?

Blockages rarely appear out of nowhere — there is usually a story behind them. The most common causes, drawing on the traditions behind chakra healing, include:

  • Emotional suppression. When an experience is too painful to process in the moment, the body archives it. Unresolved grief, anger, shame, or fear can settle near the chakra closest to where that emotion was held, disrupting its flow.
  • Chronic stress. A nervous system braced for constant threat cannot sustain the open, relaxed state healthy energy flow needs. Long-term pressure gradually restricts the lower chakras in particular.
  • Physical illness or injury. Trauma, poor diet, or disconnection from the body can disturb the chakra governing the affected area.
  • Major life transitions and inherited patterns. Loss, relocation, toxic relationships, or beliefs absorbed in childhood all leave energetic imprints that can show up as blocks years later.

The encouraging part is the reverse is also true: once you know which chakra needs attention, small consistent practices — including the right crystals — can restore the flow. Take the test above to find your starting point.

Symptoms of a Blocked Chakra (Each One Explained)

Each of the seven chakras shows distinct emotional and physical signs when blocked. The patterns below, gathered from established chakra and yoga traditions, can help you pinpoint which energy center needs care — even before taking the test.

1. Root Chakra (Muladhara)

Color: Red · Element: Earth · Location: Base of the spine

Governs safety, stability, belonging, and your right to take up space. When blocked you may feel persistent financial anxiety, scarcity thinking, a sense of being abandoned or fundamentally unwanted, restlessness, or difficulty feeling at home — even in your own body. Physical signs often include chronic lower-back, hip, or leg pain, sciatica, immune issues, or constipation. A clear root chakra feels grounded and trusting that your needs will be met.

2. Sacral Chakra (Svadhisthana)

Color: Orange · Element: Water · Location: Below the navel

The seat of creativity, pleasure, sensuality, and emotional flow. When blocked you may experience a creative drought, guilt around enjoyment, intimacy issues, repeating toxic relationship patterns, or emotional numbness that swings into drama. Physical signs can show up as reproductive or kidney concerns, pelvic or lower-back pain, and urinary issues. An open sacral chakra lets you enjoy life as something to savor.

3. Solar Plexus Chakra (Manipura)

Color: Yellow · Element: Fire · Location: Above the navel

Your center of personal power, confidence, and will. When blocked — which many experience as anger turned inward or outward — you may struggle with self-doubt, indecision, giving your power away to avoid conflict, or the opposite: control, aggression, and a need to dominate. Physical signs include digestive tension, stomach ulcers, liver strain, chronic fatigue, or blood-pressure swings. A balanced solar plexus lets you act from grounded confidence without needing approval.

4. Heart Chakra (Anahata)

Color: Green · Element: Air · Location: Center of the chest

The bridge between body and spirit, governing love, compassion, and connection. When blocked you may build emotional walls from past hurt that keep out the good along with the bad, struggle to forgive, hold calcified resentment, or feel lonely even among people who care. Physical signs can include chest tightness, shallow breathing, asthma, or upper-back and shoulder tension. An open heart gives and receives love without depletion.

5. Throat Chakra (Vishuddha)

Color: Blue · Element: Sound · Location: The throat

Governs communication, truth, and self-expression. When blocked you may swallow your truth to keep the peace, feel perpetually misunderstood, say yes when you mean no, or over-talk to compensate. Physical signs often include sore throat, thyroid imbalance, neck stiffness, laryngitis, or recurrent ear issues. An open throat chakra lets you speak honestly without calculating the risk first.

6. Third Eye Chakra (Ajna)

Color: Indigo · Element: Light · Location: Between the eyebrows

The center of intuition, insight, and clarity. When blocked you may experience brain fog, poor focus, distrust of your instincts even when they have been right, decision paralysis, or a vague sense that something is misaligned. Physical signs can include headaches, vision strain, sinus issues, sleep disruption, or hormonal imbalance. A clear third eye helps you see situations and people for what they are.

7. Crown Chakra (Sahasrara)

Color: Violet · Element: Thought · Location: Top of the head

Your connection to something greater — wisdom, purpose, and universal consciousness. When blocked you may feel a persistent existential emptiness, cynicism, disconnection, or a sense that life is mechanical and purposeless. Physical signs sometimes include brain fog, migraines, or sensitivity to light and environment. An open crown brings a felt sense of meaning and being held by a larger intelligence.

How to Balance a Blocked Chakra

Once the test reveals your most blocked chakra, the path forward blends intention with simple daily practice. The most accessible traditional methods are:

Work with Chakra Crystals

Crystals are the most practical tool for everyday chakra work, because you can carry them with you. Here's how to use them:

  • Choose the right stone for your blocked chakra (see the guide below).
  • Wear it as jewelry — a bracelet or pendant keeps the stone's energy close to your body all day, the easiest way to maintain focus on one chakra.
  • Meditate with it — place the crystal on or near the chakra's body location during quiet reflection.
  • Carry it in a pocket or bag as a tactile reminder of your intention.
  • Place it on your nightstand or desk to keep the energy in your space.

Chakra Meditation & Bija Mantras

Sit quietly, close your eyes, and focus on the blocked chakra's location and color, visualizing it becoming clear and vibrant. Each chakra also has a seed sound (bija mantra) traditionally chanted to create vibration in that center: LAM (root), VAM (sacral), RAM (solar plexus), YAM (heart), HAM (throat), OM (third eye), AH (crown). Spend 3–5 minutes per chakra, pairing the mantra with a crystal placed on the body area for a deeper session.

Move Your Body

Yoga and movement clear blocks physically — forward folds ground the root, hip openers release the sacral, twists activate the solar plexus, backbends open the heart. Time in nature, sunlight, and fluid movement like dance or swimming also restore flow where energy has stagnated.

How Accurate Is a Chakra Test?

A chakra test reflects how you feel right now, not a fixed state. Your energy shifts with stress, seasons, relationships, and intentional practice — so results can change from week to week. The most honest answers give the most useful insight. Think of the test as a mirror for where to focus your attention, paired with the crystals traditionally used to support that focus. Retake it every few weeks to track how your balance evolves.

Chakra Test FAQ

Which chakra is blocked? +

The test above tells you — it flags the energy center scoring lowest based on your honest answers. Everyone's pattern differs: one person may have a blocked root (security), another a blocked throat (expression). Take the test to find yours.

How do I know which chakra is blocked? +

Two ways: take the 14-question test above, or read the Symptoms section and notice which resonates — anxiety about money points to root, creative numbness to sacral, fear of speaking up to throat, and so on. The area of life that feels most stuck is usually where the block sits.

What happens when a chakra is blocked? +

A blocked chakra shows up as imbalance in its life area, with both emotional and physical signs — a blocked heart may feel like difficulty giving or receiving love and show up as chest tightness; a blocked solar plexus as low confidence and digestive tension. Because all seven chakras are connected, one blockage puts pressure on the rest. See the Symptoms section above for each chakra's specific signs.

Which chakra is blocked by anger? +

Most often the solar plexus chakra, which governs personal power and will. Unprocessed anger commonly settles here and can swing between two extremes — either outward aggression, control, and a need to dominate, or inward collapse into powerlessness and indecision. Citrine, pyrite, and tiger's eye are the stones traditionally used to restore balance here.

How long does it take to unblock a chakra? +

There is no fixed timeline. A recent, surface-level block can shift after a single meditation or movement session with the right crystal; a deeper one rooted in long-held patterns may take weeks or months of consistent practice. What matters is consistency rather than intensity — retake the test periodically to track progress.
